## Executive Summary — Top 10 Insights

Positive: 4 · Negative: 3 · Mixed: 3

1. **[±] All three banks trade well above their own long-run valuation, richest at DBS.** Current P/B is 3.00 for DBS (+99% vs its 10-yr average of 1.51), 2.12 for OCBC (+83%), and 1.53 for UOB (+31%), so the re-rating is real but leaves little historical cushion. (Table 4 — Current P/B / Current vs 10-yr avg rows)

2. **[+] DBS compounded profit fastest over the decade and leads on returns.** DBS net-profit CAGR was 11.2% (FY2016→25) and 12.9% (FY2021→25), reaching S$11.4bn in FY2024, against a 10-yr average ROE of 13.4% versus 11.2% (OCBC) and 10.9% (UOB). (Table 1 — DBS; Table 4 — 10-yr avg ROE row)

3. **[–] UOB's FY2025 net profit fell 23% on a large pre-emptive provision.** The drop reflects ~S$2.0bn of general allowances booked in 3Q2025, cutting reported ROE to 9.6% while operating profit was only −4%. (Table 1 — UOB footnote 5; Appendix C)

4. **[+] The 2022–23 rate cycle drove net interest margin to a decade peak across the group.** Group NIM topped out in FY2023 at 2.15% (DBS), 2.28% (OCBC), and 2.09% (UOB), lifting NII sharply as 3M SORA rose toward 3.74 at end-2023. (Table 3; Table 5)

5. **[–] That margin tailwind is now reversing as rates fall.** FY2025 group NIM slipped to 2.01% (DBS), 1.91% (OCBC), and 1.89% (UOB) as 3M SORA (31-Dec) fell from 3.74 in 2023 to 1.26 in 2025 and 1.05 by mid-2026. (Table 5)

6. **[+] DBS has built the strongest capital-light wealth flywheel.** DBS wealth AUM compounded 12.7% p.a. from S$166bn in 2016 to S$488bn in 2025, helping non-NII reach S$8.4bn (of which wealth-management income was S$5.7bn) in FY2025. (Table 1 — DBS non-NII note; Table 2)

7. **[±] DBS deposit quality has eroded even as balances grew.** DBS CASA fell from 76.0% in 2021 to 51.8% in 2024 before edging to 54.5% in 2025, so cheap, sticky funding shrank as a share while total deposits still rose to S$610bn. (Table 2)

8. **[+] UOB's funding mix has re-strengthened post-rate-cycle.** UOB CASA lifted from about 48% to 58% between 2023 and 2025 on a deposit remix out of fixed deposits plus the deposit-heavy Citi consumer book. (Table 2 — footnote u1)

9. **[±] OCBC gathers assets more slowly and carries more insurance-linked earnings volatility.** OCBC wealth AUM CAGR was 4.2% (2018→25), lagging DBS and UOB, and Great Eastern earnings are exposed to interest-rate and equity mark-to-market swings that forced an SFRS(I) 17 restatement of FY2022. (Table 1 — OCBC non-NII note; Appendix C)

10. **[–] Several inputs remain model-correlated and low-confidence, warranting spot-verification.** Both prior data passes were Claude Opus 4.8 and several AUM/CASA cells are single-retriever (`single-px`), so the report advises spot-checking un-checksummed cells against Tier-1 sources before high-stakes use. (Appendix A — Provenance caveat; `n/r`/`n/d` inventory)
