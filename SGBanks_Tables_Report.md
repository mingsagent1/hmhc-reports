### Why deposits + CASA is the attraction benchmark

Customer **deposits** are the primary asset-attraction proxy: the only attraction measure all three banks disclose every year FY2016–2025, on a standardised basis, and here dual-verified (Perplexity + Tier-1 deposit notes agree). They are on-balance-sheet client money and exclude leverage. **Total assets** is rejected as the attraction base — it is inflated by wholesale-funded leverage, so it measures balance-sheet size, not assets attracted (it belongs in the leverage lens, Total assets ÷ deposits). **CASA is the quality overlay**: deposit size can be flattered by buying deposits with high fixed-deposit rates, so CASA isolates the cheap, sticky, relationship money — a bank whose deposits grow while CASA falls is paying up, not attracting (caveat: rate-cycle sensitive). **Wealth AUM** is the truest flywheel but a secondary overlay (patchy pre-2019, bank-specific definitions). Deposits and AUM are never summed (double-count risk); each pool is paired with its own income instead (NII/deposits, fees/AUM).

## Appendix B — Definitions

- **NIM (group):** each bank's printed group net interest margin. DBS publishes three NIMs; the group series is used throughout (the commercial-book series, 2.80% in FY2024, is excluded).
- **Wealth-management income:** not comparable across banks — DBS and OCBC embed NII on wealth deposits; UOB is narrower and was restated 1-Jan-2023. Reported as printed, never treated as a slice of non-II.
- **Net-fee basis:** DBS on a commercial-book basis (recent years); OCBC and UOB on a group basis.
- **ROE / RoTE:** as reported by each bank. UOB core-ROE (ex-one-offs) disclosed for FY2023 (~14.2%) only; OCBC/UOB do not print RoTE.
- **CustomerDeposits:** group non-bank customer deposits (balance-sheet liability).
- **TotalAssets:** consolidated total assets (balance-sheet total); "net" definition to be confirmed by owner (default = total assets as printed).
- **WealthAUM:** reported wealth / private-bank AUM (off-balance-sheet) — DBS "Wealth Management AUM"; OCBC group/banking wealth AUM (incl. Bank of Singapore + Great Eastern); UOB wealth AUM. Definitions differ; not strictly comparable across banks.
- **CASA ratio:** (current + savings) ÷ total customer deposits — bank-printed where available, else computed from the deposit note.
